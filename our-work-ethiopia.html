Closing the Service Gap: Spotlight on Ethiopia

Despite progress in healthcare and education coverage, Ethiopia still faces significant challenges in reaching its most vulnerable populations – including children, youth, women, and elderly people. The Federal Ministry of Health, in collaboration with the Ministry of Education and Ministry of Women and Social Affairs, has outlined a comprehensive plan to address this challenge, including:

  • Improving education and sensitization of key health, education, and protection information among vulnerable families
  • Improving HIV management and support for all affected age groups, including prevention of mother‑to‑child transmission and care for elderly guardians
  • Identifying the ‘last mile’ of underserved communities – children out of school, unvaccinated children, elderly without social protection, and families facing food insecurity

Our Partnerships

Providing technical assistance to in-country partners in Ethiopia

EKFcharity began a year‑long pilot in Ethiopia in 2021, working with the Federal Ministry of Health, the Ministry of Women and Social Affairs, and the Clinton Health Access Initiative (CHAI) to test GROW as a cost‑effective tool for reaching vulnerable families with essential health, education, and protection information. The combination of mobile health (mHealth) and sourcing information directly from vulnerable households offers a unique approach to identifying underserved children, youth, women, and elderly people in areas with high service coverage. The pilot was successful, proving GROW could drive positive care‑seeking behaviors while generating granular data to support government priorities. The Ministry has since committed to scale GROW country‑wide to improve access to health services, increase school enrollment and retention, strengthen child protection reporting, and support national efforts around HIV prevention and support for all vulnerable groups.

Scaling up nationally in Ethiopia

GROW is deployed in pilot regions across the country, from Tigray in the north to Southern Nations, Nationalities, and Peoples' Region (SNNPR) in the south. EKFcharity is working with the Federal Ministry of Health, the Ministry of Women and Social Affairs, and CHAI to deepen its scale and impact in these regions by partnering with public health centers, schools, and social service points to enroll vulnerable families to the platform, with the aim of reaching all regions by 2025.

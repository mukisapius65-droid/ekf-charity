CHALLENGE WE'RE ADDRESSING

A skills‑building solution to close gaps in child protection, psychosocial support, and emergency response.

A key driver of vulnerability is that community volunteers and social workers often lack the training and confidence to handle complex cases – from child abuse to mental health crises. In Uganda, skill gaps lead to inconsistent care, missed protections, and burnout. Empowering frontline workers with practical, hands‑on training is critical: over 80% of volunteers report feeling unprepared for child protection cases before FIELDS.

HOW FIELDS WORKS

FIELDS is a structured training and quality assurance platform that builds the capacity of community volunteers, social workers, and local leaders through simulation‑based learning, continuous assessment, and certification.

A suite of training tools that builds confidence and competence for frontline workers.

Modular, context‑specific curriculum

Covers child protection protocols, psychosocial first aid, HIV sensitization, livelihood coaching, and emergency response – tailored to Uganda’s community realities.

Skill gap tracking & certification

Our platform tracks each volunteer’s progress, identifies knowledge gaps, and issues verifiable certificates – ensuring quality and accountability.

Real‑time supervisor dashboards

District managers can monitor volunteer performance, case outcomes, and training completion – enabling data‑driven support and resource allocation.

FIELDS is designed to be scaled and sustained within government social protection systems.

From the beginning, we designed FIELDS to have low delivery costs – using local trainers, mobile‑first content, and peer learning – so that district governments can integrate it into their annual budgets. Today, 15% of FIELDS operating costs are covered by district local governments, and we partner with the Ministry of Gender and social welfare departments to embed training into national child protection frameworks.

Free for community volunteers

All training materials and certification are provided at no cost to volunteers.

Low cost per trainee

Under $12 per volunteer for full curriculum and certification.

Affordable For Governments

15% of running costs covered by district governments – target 50% by 2027.

Scalable to new districts

Active in 22 districts; partnerships with 3 national ministries (Gender, Health, Education).

FIELDS in action

When 12‑year‑old Sarah was withdrawn and missing school, her mother reached out to a community volunteer trained by FIELDS.

The volunteer knew exactly what to do.

Using psychosocial support techniques learned in FIELDS, the volunteer built trust, identified signs of abuse, and made a confidential referral to child protection services. Sarah is now back in school and receiving counseling – and the volunteer has become a local child protection champion.

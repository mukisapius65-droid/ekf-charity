CHALLENGE WE'RE ADDRESSING

A digital solution to address delays in accessing health, education, and protection services.

A key driver of vulnerability is that vulnerable families including children, youth, women, and elderly people are not empowered or informed to seek healthcare, educational support, or child protection services at the appropriate time. In Uganda, delays in care seeking and service access contribute significantly to poor health outcomes, school dropouts, and unaddressed protection risks. Empowering communities with knowledge is critical: over 85% of vulnerable families would seek support if they knew how and when to access available services.

OUR MODEL

HOW GROW WORKS

An AI‑powered two‑way SMS service that empowers vulnerable families to seek help at the right time and place. Users receive personalised messages (health alerts, education reminders, protection tips) and can ask questions via a helpdesk. If a risk is detected, we trigger an immediate referral to health workers, social workers, or emergency relief.

A suite of digital tools that supports vulnerable families to seek care and amplifies their voices to improve its quality.

A sequence of messages

Tracked to a family’s specific needs (child’s age, pregnancy stage, school calendar, or elderly care schedule) influencing key behaviors linked with better outcomes, like prenatal care attendance, child vaccination, school enrollment, HIV testing, and nutrition for the elderly.

An AI‑enabled helpdesk

That reads, triages, and responds to families’ questions about health, education, child protection, and livelihoods and sparks a rapid referral chain if a risk (health crisis, protection concern, or food insecurity) is identified during the exchange.

A comprehensive data infrastructure

That collects and aggregates data from vulnerable families to help our district and national government partners improve the quality of health, education, child protection, and social services and strengthen systems for the most marginalized.

GROW is designed to be scaled and sustained within government social support and health systems.

From the beginning, we designed GROW to have low unit costs so that it can sustainably scale and integrate within government work plans and budgets as a pathway to long‑term sustainability. District local governments across Uganda are increasingly contributing towards the platform’s running costs, and we work with other partners like local telecoms to achieve economies of scale as we grow. The platform is built with and for vulnerable families to advance equity, uptake, and adoption, and relies on locally‑developed technology that scales and adapts easily to new contexts and languages (including Luganda, Runyankole, and other local languages) with minimal enhancements.

Free For Volunarable Families

Users sign up to GROW for free at community health centers, schools, and social service points.

Low Unit Costs

Under $0.80 lifetime cost per vulnerable family enrolled in GROW.

Affordable For Governments

15% of GROW operating costs are covered by district local governments across Uganda, with more committing each year.

Scalable To New Contexts

3 active partnerships with national Ministries (Health, Education, and Gender) to scale GROW across Uganda and into neighboring countries.

GROW in action

When, midway through pregnancy, Grace noticed she was bleeding, she messaged GROW to ask if this was normal.

It was a lifeline.

Within minutes, the AI‑enabled helpdesk flagged her risk. A community health worker called her, arranged an immediate clinic visit, and followed up daily. Grace delivered a healthy baby girl and now trains other pregnant women in her village to use GROW.
